ACCENT

1. Must be on one of the last three syllables.

N.B.— -οι -αι are counted as short for this purpose, except Opt. mood.

2. If the last syllable is long, must be on one of the last two.

3. In Nouns, Adj., and Part., it remains on the same syllabic as in Nom., provided this does not break Rule 2.

But—

(a) Monosyllables of the 3rd declension, accent final in Gen. and Dat.

(b) Gen. Pl. of 1st declension has the circumflex on the last.

4. In Verbs, it goes back as far as the general rules (1 and 2) allow.

But—

(a) Strong or 2nd Aor. accents last in Part. and Inf. Act.

(b) All Aorists Passive accent last in Part., last but one in Inf.

(c) Perf. Pass, accents last but one in Part. and Inf.

5. (a) Enclitics throw back an acute on the preceding word, unless that word has the acute on its last syllable but one, or the circumflex on its last.

(b) Disyllabic enclitics take acute on their last syllable if the preceding word has the acute on its last but one.

N.B.—Names of words with various accents:—

ἐγώ, oxytone
λόγος, paroxytone
ἕτερος, proparoxytone
ὧν, perispomenon
τῶνδε, properispomenon
